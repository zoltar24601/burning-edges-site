DIY Solar Rock Fountain & Bird Bath

By Shannon · A natural-looking fountain the birds love — no wiring, no plumbing, the sun runs the whole thing.

What you'll need

  • A pile of large rocks — about $12 at a landscaping supplier

How to build it

  1. Pick a sunny spot

    Solar pumps need direct sun to run, so choose the location before you fill anything — somewhere the birds will feel safe stopping by.

  2. Set your planter in place

    Once it's full of rocks and water it's heavy to move, so position it first.

  3. Rinse the rocks

    A quick hose-down keeps the water clear and clean for the birds.

  4. Place the solar pump

    Set the pump in the bottom center of the planter where it'll sit under the water.

  5. Build up the rocks

    Stack rocks around and over the pump for that natural look, leaving the pump's spout peeking out so the water can bubble up through the stones.

  6. Fill with water

    Add water to just below the top of your rock arrangement, enough to cover the pump.

  7. Let the sun do the rest

    The fountain starts as soon as the sun hits it. Top off the water every few days as it evaporates.

Bird tip: keep a few top rocks shallow and flat so birds have a place to perch and drink, not just splash.
